Meaty Pasta Sauce

Description

There are a billion ways to make a pasta sauce, but this is my favorite so far. It takes a long damn time and has to be made in these particular steps to fully develop the "depth of flavor". I've broken down the steps into various "Phases" so that you can divide the labor if you have helpers. You will need a Dutch Oven or similar sized pot. Makes 8 ample servings

Ingredients:

  • 1 Lb Ground Beef
  • 1 LB Ground Pork
  • 1 LB Italian Sausages (sweet, mild or spicy your choice)
  • 2 Large Carrots
  • 4 Stalks of Celery
  • 1 Whole Onion (Red onion preferred, but any work)
  • 1 Whole Bell Pepper (Red, Yellow or Green)
  • 1 Whole Zucchini or Squash or both even
  • 8oz of Mushrooms
  • 1 Whole Bulb of Garlic
  • 8 Whole Tomatoes (or 2 cans of whole, peeled, or diced tomatoes)
  • 1 big can of traditional pasta sauce, OR 2 small cans/jars
  • 2 Tablespoons of Tomato Paste
  • 4 Cups or Stock or Broth (Beef, Chicken, Bone or Vegetable whatever you have)
  • 1 Cup of Wine (again anything works if not too sweet or too dry)
  • 2 Tablespoons Worchestersire Sauce
  • 2 Tablespoons Balsalmic Vinnegar
  • 2 Tablespoons Italian Seasoning
  • 1 Teaspoon Salt
  • 1 Tablespoon Black Pepper
  • 1 Teaspoon of Brown Sugar
  • 1 Bunch of Fresh Parcely or 2 Tablespoons of Dried Parcely
  • 3oz Fresh Grated Parmesean Cheese
  • 2oz Fresh or grated Mozzarella Cheese
  • 1 LB of Dry Pasta
  • A few splashes of Olive Oil

Instructions:

  1. Chopping Phase! Get started on this step Early and do all your knife-work in one phase while keeping the cut ingredients seperated until the Saute Phase.
  2. Dice up your Carrots as fine as you please
  3. Slice your Cellery Sticks as fine as you like
  4. Dice your Onion to your preference
  5. Remove the stalk and seeds of your Bell Pepper and give it a good dicing
  6. Wash and medium dice your Zucchini and/or Squash
  7. Wash and slice your Mushrooms to your preference
  8. Wash your Tomatoes and cut out the stem, then dice them up if using fresh tomatoes, or just open the can if using canned tomatoes
  9. Peel then slice or dice all your garlic the way you like it
  10. Finely chop the Fresh Parcely
  11. Grate or shred your Parmesean and Mozzarella Cheeses
  12. Lastly slice your Italian sausages into 1/2 inch thick slices, then cut the slices in half
  13. Wash your cutting board, you are done with it now.
  14. Searing Phase!
  15. Place the Italian Sausage into a cold Dutch oven and turn the Heat to Medium High. Allow them to render thier own fat to start frying. Cook turning every 3 minutes until the pieces devolop a solid sear, just don't burn them.
  16. Add the Ground Pork to the frying Italian Sausages, Breaking up chunks and mixing every 2 Minutes until 1/2 browned
  17. Add the Ground Beef to the mix, Continuing to break up chunks and sear untill all the meats are well browned
  18. Strain off the excess grease and save for later
  19. Remove the Browned Meats and set asside
  20. Deglaze and Saute' Phase!Greasy Pot with Browned Bits (Fond) at the bottom returned to Medium-Low Heat
  21. Add about a Tablespoon of the Reserved Meat Grease to the Pot
  22. Add the Diced Carrots and Celery to the Pot and cook for about 10 min stirring occasionally and scraping up the Fond from the bottom of the pot
  23. Add the Diced Onions and Stir occasionally Continuing to work the Fond from the bottom until the Onions become translucent
  24. Now Add the Garlic and cook for 2 Minutes until Fragrant
  25. Turn the Heat up to Medium-High and add the Tomato Paste and cook for about 3 minutes to get a little sear on the Paste
  26. At this point it should be getting kinda thick and pasty in the bottom of the Pot
  27. Add the Wine while working the bottom of the pot with a flat edged wooden spoon for about 2 Minutes
  28. Add the Mushrooms and cook for about 2 minutes
  29. Now add the Bell Pepper and Zucchini/Squash and cook for about 3 minutes stirring occasionally
  30. Now add the Tomatoes and break them up with a wooden spoon, or if using canned tomatoes, just pour them in
  31. Now add the reserved Meat, The Stock or Broth, Worchestersire Sauce, Balsalmic Vinnegar, Italian Seasoning, Salt, Pepper and Brown Sugar
  32. Simmering Phase!Bring the Sauce to a boil then reduce heat to Low and Cover the Pot
  33. Allow the Sauce to simmer for at least 2 hours, stirring occasionally and scraping the bottom of the pot to keep the Fond from burning
  34. After 2 hours, taste the sauce and adjust the seasoning to your liking
  35. Allow the sauce to simmer for another 1-2 hours, stirring occasionally and scraping the bottom of the pot
  36. When the sauce is done, remove it from the heat and stir in the Fresh Parcely, Parmesean and Mozzarella Cheeses
  37. Allow the sauce to cool for a few minutes before serving
  38. Cook your Pasta according to the package instructions, then drain and toss with olive oil to prevent sticking
  39. Serve the Sauce over the Pasta, garnished with additional Fresh Parcely and Parmesean Cheese if desired
